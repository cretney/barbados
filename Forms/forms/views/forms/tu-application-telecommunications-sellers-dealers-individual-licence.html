Introduction


This document contains general information and guidelines for interested parties to apply for telecommunication Sellers and Dealer’s licence.

Importers, retailers, manufacturers intending to manufacture, import, sell, deal radio telecommunication equipment may apply for the Sellers and Dealer’s Licence. One of the requirements is that it must be a company incorporated or a business registered with the Corporate Affairs and Intellectual Property Office (CAIPO).

There are two types of Telecommunication Sellers and Dealer’s Licence; namely

(a)

(b)
Telecommunication Sellers and Dealer’s Individual Licence; and

Telecommunication Sellers and Dealer’s Class Licence.

Definition

“Dealer” means a person who manufactures, imports, sells, hires, offers or possess for sale, hire or hire-purchase any equipment which is capable for being used for the purpose of telecommunication, and includes his employees.

“sales” includes every transaction of or the nature of barter of exchange, every transaction of hire or hire-purchase and every transaction by which goods are pawned for hypothecated or as a result of which the ownership of or an interest in goods is or may be transferred or created.

“Telecommunication Sellers and Dealer’s Licence” means a Sellers and Dealer’s Class Licence granted under the Telecommunications Act 2001-36.

“telecommunication equipment “ means any appliance, apparatus or accessory used or intended to be used for telecommunication.

“specified equipment” means any telecommunication equipment approved, used or intended to be used for telecommunication.

“Act” means the Telecommunications Act 2001-36.

“CAIPO” means Corporate Affairs and Intellectual Property Office.

Scope of Licence

Sellers and Dealer’s Licence

(a)


(b)

(c)
No person, other than a holder of a Sellers and Dealer’s Individual Licence granted by the Ministry, shall import, hire, sell, or offer of possess for sale, any telecommunication equipment other than type-approved equipment or telecommunication equipment.

A holder of a Sellers and Dealer’s Individual Licence shall not sell prohibited telecommunication equipment.

A Seller and Dealer shall obtain a Dealer’s Individual Licence for each of the premises under his control or occupation at which he imports, hires, sells, offers or possesses for sale any telecommunication equipment.

Sellers and Dealer’s Class Licence

(a)
A Seller and Dealer who imports, hires, sells or offers or possesses for sale any specified equipment or telecommunication equipment set out in Appendix 3 ($1,200 for sales more than $2,000, $600 for sales less than $2,000) shall be deemed to have been granted a Sellers and Dealer’s Class Licence for that purpose.

Sellers and Dealer’s Class Licence Conditions

In these conditions, “relevant telecommunication equipment” means the telecommunication equipment imported, hired, sold, offered or possessed for sale by the holder of a Sellers and Dealer’s Licence.

(a)


(b)


(c)

A holder of a Sellers and Dealer’s Class Licence if required by the Ministry, produce at his expense any relevant telecommunication equipment for inspection by the Ministry.

A holder of a Sellers and Dealer’s Class Licence shall not have in his possession:

(a)

(b)

(c)
any telecommunication equipment that is prohibited equipment;

any device or equipment that is capable of monitoring or intercepting the transmissions of a radio-communication network; or

any device or equipment that can be used to insert or duplicate the identification of an authorised telecommunication equipment to an unauthorised telecommunication equipment.

6.

A holder of a Sellers and Dealer’s Class Licence shall comply with the Ministry’s policies and directions in relation to the relevant telecommunication equipment as specified from time to time.

This Licence is issued by the Ministry responsible for Telecommunications in Barbados in accordance with and subject to the Act and shall remain valid for the duration of this Licence, unless revoked by the Minister in accordance with Section 62 of the Act.

The Licence fee is $600 for sales under $2,000 or $1,200 for sales over sales over $2,000 per annum, and shall be payable in advance and is not refundable. Notwithstanding the grant of this Licence, the Licensee shall obtain the Ministry’s prior approval for the import of any telecommunication equipment and shall comply with any terms and conditions which may be imposed by the Ministry thereof. The import of scanning receivers, military communication equipment, telephone voice changing equipment, automatic call diverters, radio-communication equipment operating in frequency bands 886-901 MHz and 931-950 MHz (except cellular mobile phones or such other equipment approved by the Ministry) is prohibited.

The Licensee shall import, hire, sell or offer or possess for sale only:

(b)

The Licensee shall, when required, furnish the Ministry with such detailed specifications and information on the types of telecommunication equipment sold or to be sold by the licensee.

The Licensee shall not have in his possession any device or equipment which is capable of monitoring or intercepting the transmission of a radio-communication network or which can be used to alter, insert, delete, duplicate or remove the ESN, MIN or PIN number of a registered cellular mobile telephone to an unregistered mobile telephone.

The Licensee shall not sell prohibited telecommunication equipment.

The Licensee shall

The Licensee shall maintain a record of every sale of any type of telecommunication equipment, as per section 56 paragraph 5 to 7 of the Telecommunications Act 2001-36.

Where necessary, the Licensee shall inform every purchaser or hirer to whom he sells or lets any telecommunication equipment of the need to obtain a licence from the Ministry under the Act for the possession, operation, installation or use of such telecommunication equipment.

Notwithstanding the grant of this Licence, the Licensee shall obtain the Ministry’s approval for the operation of any telecommunication equipment for demonstration purposes.

The Minister may modify, suspend, revoke or refuse to renew a licence in accordance with section 62 of the Act. The Licensee shall, at his own expense, comply with the varied conditions.

The Licensee shall ensure that his employees comply with the provisions of the Act.

The Licensee shall comply with any directions and notifications, which the Ministry may, from time to time, issue.

Nothing in this Licence shall render the Ministry liable or responsible for any infringement by a Licensee, of copyright in any work or any patent or for any breach of any written law by the Licensee.

The Ministry shall have the power to take action against the Licensee under the Act and to suspend or revoke this Licence by notice in writing where the Licensee:-

(b)

fails to comply with any of the conditions of this Licence, any provision of any code of practice, standard of performance or any direction of the Ministry.
